**Investment Thesis**: The debate crystallizes around one asymmetry: the fundamental improvement at BILI is real, but the market is pricing what comes next, not what just happened. The Bull's strongest argument—net cash representing ~25% of market cap—provides genuine downside protection and is the sole reason this is an Underweight rather than a Sell. A business that swung from negative 7.5B CNY net income to positive 1.19B CNY, with gross margins doubling to 37.1% and 30% ad growth, is not a broken enterprise. However, the Bear won on three points the Bull could not adequately counter. First, growth velocity has halved: Q1's annualized ~6.7% revenue growth versus FY2025's 13.1% is a structural deceleration, not noise, and the high-margin ad business is not filling the gap fast enough as legacy game revenue compresses. Second, the profitability inflection was engineered through finite cost-cutting: R&D slashed from 21.8% to 11.6% of revenue and S&M from 22.5% to 14.5% represent harvesting, not planting—and the Q1 deceleration may be the first symptom. Third, the technical damage is unequivocal: 13.5M shares traded on May 19 produced no sustained rally, price sits 12% below the $20.03 VWMA with every recent buyer underwater, the death cross is widening, MACD just recrossed bearishly, RSI is making new lows with zero bullish divergence, and price is below the lower Bollinger band. This is institutional distribution, not retail capitulation. The Neutral Analyst's refinements are well-taken: the Conservative's $16.90 stop is too tight for a $0.99 ATR stock and would get triggered on normal volatility, while the Aggressive's "hold everything and wait" underestimates the trend's persistence. The macro overlay—IEA oil "red zone" warning, China as largest oil importer, consumer inflation risk, and the persistent China ADR discount—adds headwinds that Q1's 30% ad growth data predates. The stock is not a Sell because the net cash floor and genuine profitability keep the value-trap from becoming a value-destruction story, but at 45x P/E with growth halving, broken technicals, and building macro risk, this is absolutely not a Buy or a comfortable Hold at benchmark weight. Reduce exposure now, preserve the core for a multi-quarter recovery if it materializes, and let price action—not narrative—dictate when to re-engage.
