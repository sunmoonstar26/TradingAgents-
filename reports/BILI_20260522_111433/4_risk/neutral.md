But your “sell at least half and tighten stop to $16.90” is overly reactive. China is an oil importer, but the impact on consumer spending is lagged and uncertain. The IEA’s “red zone” warning is for July—we’re in May. De-escalation talks are ongoing. Trading on “could” cuts both ways.

So where does that leave the trader’s Hold at $17.58 with a $16 stop? I think it’s a reasonable moderate starting point, but I’d challenge both extremes to refine it.

To the aggressive analyst: You say holding now is the “most aggressive prudent action.” I disagree. The market may or may not give us that confirmation, but until it does, the path of least resistance is down. Holding is fine, but holding with a clear plan to adjust based on price action is better than either the aggressive “hold and hope” or the conservative “sell and flee.”
